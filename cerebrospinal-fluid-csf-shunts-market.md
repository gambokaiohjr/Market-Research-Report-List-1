<p><p>The cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) shunts market is categorized into fixed and adjustable shunts. Fixed CSF shunts are set to a predetermined pressure level, providing stable drainage but limited flexibility for patient-specific needs. In contrast, adjustable CSF shunts allow for pressure modulation after implantation, enabling tailored management of CSF flow according to the patient’s developing condition. This adaptability can enhance treatment outcomes and reduce complications, making adjustable shunts increasingly prominent in neurosurgical applications.</p></p>
