<p><p>The peptic ulcer testing market encompasses various diagnostic methods to identify ulcers and their underlying causes. Blood tests are used to detect antibodies against Helicobacter pylori, a common culprit. ELISA tests offer a more specific antibody detection. The urea breath test measures carbon dioxide in the breath after ingestion of a urea solution, indicating H. pylori infection. Stool tests detect the presence of H. pylori antigens or blood in stool. Other methods may include endoscopy and imaging techniques, broadening diagnostic options.</p></p>
