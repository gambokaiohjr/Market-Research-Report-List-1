<p><p>Ball screws are vital components in robotics, enabling precise linear motion. The market is characterized by two main types: internal circulation and external circulation ball screws. Internal circulation ball screws feature a closed-loop design where balls move within the screw's nut, providing compactness and reducing the risk of contamination. In contrast, external circulation ball screws have an open-loop design, allowing for easier maintenance and higher load capacities, making them suitable for larger robotic applications. Each type offers distinct advantages based on specific operational needs.</p></p>
