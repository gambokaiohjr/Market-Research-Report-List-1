<p><p>The market for boat toilets comprises two primary types: electric and manual. Electric toilets utilize a battery or shore power to flush waste, offering convenience and efficiency with minimal physical effort. They often include features like macerators for finer waste disposal. Manual toilets, on the other hand, rely on a hand-pump mechanism, making them simpler and often less expensive. Both types cater to diverse boating preferences, emphasizing ease of use, maintenance, and space considerations, while ensuring effective waste management on the water.</p></p>
