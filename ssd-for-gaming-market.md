<p><p>The SSD gaming market features two primary types: Flash and DRAM SSDs. Flash SSDs, using NAND technology, offer high-speed storage with faster loading times and improved game performance, making them ideal for gamers seeking efficiency and reduced latency. DRAM SSDs, on the other hand, incorporate DRAM cache for enhanced read/write speeds, particularly beneficial for data-heavy applications. Both types cater to various gaming needs, with Flash SSDs focusing on affordability and capacity, while DRAM SSDs prioritize speed and performance.</p></p>
