<p><p>In the automotive market, aluminum is categorized into three main types: cast, rolled, and extruded. </p><p>Cast aluminum is used for complex shapes and engine components, providing strength and lightweight characteristics. Rolled aluminum is utilized for sheets and panels, ideal for body structures due to its malleability and durability. Extruded aluminum is produced by forcing material through a die, yielding versatile profiles for framework and support elements. Each type enhances performance, fuel efficiency, and design flexibility in modern vehicles.</p></p>
