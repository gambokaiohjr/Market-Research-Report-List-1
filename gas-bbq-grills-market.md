<p><p>The gas BBQ grills market consists primarily of two types: Liquid Propane (LP) BBQ grills and Natural Gas (NG) BBQ grills. Liquid Propane grills utilize compressed gas stored in portable tanks, offering flexibility and mobility for outdoor cooking. In contrast, Natural Gas grills are connected directly to a home’s gas line, providing a continuous supply of fuel without the need for refilling tanks. Both types cater to different consumer preferences, fueling widespread adoption in residential and recreational settings.</p></p>
