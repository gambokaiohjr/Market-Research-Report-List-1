<p><ul><li>Analog Consumer Audio Codecs</li><li>Digital Consumer Audio Codecs</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The consumer audio codec market is divided into two main types: analog and digital codecs. Analog consumer audio codecs process continuous signals, typically found in traditional audio equipment like vinyl players and cassette decks, preserving the original sound waveforms. In contrast, digital consumer audio codecs convert audio signals into digital data, enabling compression and enhanced playback features in modern devices such as smartphones, streaming services, and digital audio players. This distinction reflects varying technological approaches to audio reproduction and transmission.</p></p>
